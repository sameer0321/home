Education on the history of Democratic Kampuchea

At least 1.7 million Cambodians died and many more suffered during the Pol Pot regime of Democratic Kampuchea. From April 17, 1975 to January 6, 1979, the Khmer Rouge carried out a totalitarian communist regime that transformed Cambodian society in all areas, including the destruction of culture, traditions, languages, the killing of intellectuals, and the transformation of the country into a nation. A great agricultural economy. The revolution brought with it killings, overwork, starvation, disease, and family breakdown. The perpetrators of the genocide were senior cadres who carried out a strict secret service like Pol Pot and other top political cadres. This political system collapsed after the presence of the Vietnamese army in 1979.

Prey Veng province has a high population density that was oppressed by the Khmer Rouge atrocities. The link between the US-Vietnam War and the military invasion caused great suffering. In addition, the assassination and forced evacuation of So Phim, the region's top commander, carried out a devastating agricultural reform plan during 1977. So Phim committed suicide in 1978 due to a dispute with the organization.
